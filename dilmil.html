Dil Mil App Re-Design

Dating App for the Indian community in US

Description

Trying to solve the problems in the applications that were very prominent while using the app.

  • Tools Used: Affinity Designer | Affinity Photo | inVision | Sketch
  • Softwares Used: LucidCharts | Coggle
Existing Problem

1. Redesign to allow the users to learn more about the potential match visually rather than current text-heavy profile detailed screen.
2. If the user is connected the Instagram account to their Dil Mil profile we can show a preview of their Instagram images upfront than an Instagram button on their profile.
3. If the user has mutual friends than we might be able to showcase them upfront rather than a mutual friend button on their profile.

Goals of the Project

Goal of the project is to solve the given above problems and to create a better User experience fot the users using the application.

Direction to Solution

1. Going for a Human Centered Design and Lean UX Design approach.
2. Chalking down research questions, Ideation.
3. Making profiles to know who the end users will be.
4. Writing down screener, pre-test and post-test questionnaires.
5. Taking interviews of participants of participants.
6. Making affinity diagrams (LucidCharts), personas (Sketch, Affinity Designer, Affinity Photo), storyboarding (Pencil, Paper), mind mapping (Coggle).
7. Chalking out the sitemaps (Sketch, Affinity Designer).
8. Sketching low-fi wireframes using paper and pencil.
9. Setting style guides to make the wireframes using Sketch or Affinity Designer.
10. Making wireframes in Axure RP8.
11. Setting style guide for high fi prototype to know typography, color palette.
12. Taking a small test to know if the app is user-friendly and if it meets the expectations with the help of Guerrilla User Tests.
13. Make the changes in the designs as per suggestions.
14. Prototyping the final design using InVision Studio app.
15. Branding the project.

Step One

Used Human Centered Design and Lean UX Design approach.
To understand user needs through investigative and generative methods and tools.
Prepared Screener questions to know more about the participants and if they can be interviewed.
Conducted interviews of various participants from different age groups, Indian people using the app.
Primary audience are people above the age of 20
They have used some sort of dating applications in the past
Interviewed 4 participants all males
Research Questions:
To help me understand the goal/problem statement I formed a research question
1. How to help people see mutual friends and instagram images without too many clicks?
2. How to reduce excess of information on the profile screen?
These are some initial thoughts that I had before diving into user research:
1. What kind of design do people go to to view mutual friends and instagram images?
2. Do they care about the location of the section?
3. Do they like certain design more than others? If so, why?
4. How do they know if a design or location of the design satisfies their needs?

Step Two

Contextual enquiry - Affinity diagram.
Interviews can highlight key user frustrations and feature requirements. The data collected can help back the design decisions later on. The interviews were structured in nature as the problem statement was already given to hence there was no need to ask open ended questions to get answers from the participants or to find out problems in the app.
Participants also performed a small task. Used affinity diagrams (Lucid Charts) to analyze data and figure out user goals and frustrations. All the important points made by participants during the interviews were grouped according to the participants. Eliminated repetitions and put post it notes under sub-categories that suited a particular need. These sub-categories then formed a category that would help define a persona and scenario.

Assumptions

Business:
Dil Mil is a match making dating application. This application would make money from extra likes, messaging, boosting, seeing mutual friends, rewinding last viewed or reseting dislikes and setting locations. As more people use the app, the app makes more money through premium memberships.
Technology:
Application used for liking other peoples profile to get matches. Progressive application - Loads instantly, responds quickly to user interactions and feels like a natural app. Has different options to explore the community and is more customizable than other applications in the market.

Step Three

Mind mapping.
Mind mapping to understand the entire process and answers the What, How and Why questions.

Step Four

Building personas and user profiles.
Personas help us a get a better understanding of the end user. For this exercise, I decided to focus on three primary personas based on the data from the affinity diagrams. The personas also highlight key

characteristics, goals and frustrations.

Step Five

Story boarding.
Created a storyboard for one scenario to show the user flow. Emoji characters, throughout the scenarios this emoji will be used to show the experience of the Dil Mil app, will play the scenarios. There will be three scenarios and storyboards in all. All the scenarios represent the problems users face while using the Dil Mil app.
Scenario One:
Problem
The user wants to check out a profile to swipe people so that he/she can get to meet new people around him/her. User sees too much information on the screen. User wants to see less information on the screen and an easier way to grasp the information then to read it all the way through.
Solution
A short 30 seconds video will be a better option that gives all the vital information about the user.
Scenario Two:
Problem
The user wants to see mutual friends in the user profile to get to know more about the person already. User sees small-darkened circles as mutual friends and not much labels to indicate so. User wants big images of the mutual friends with label as mutual friends and names of the mutual friends as well.
Solution
Big circles filled with mutual friend’s images with their names below it as well as mutual friend label on the top.
Scenario Three:
Problem
The user wants to see instagram images in the user profile to get to know more about how the person socially, or to know more about the person’s likes dislikes or to know about a person overall. User sees all the images from the app’s carousel as well as images from the Instagram together without any separation or labels confuses him and no way to visit that person’s profile either. User wants to have a separate instagram image section separate from the app’s carousel or video and a button to let the user visit the other user’s Instagram profile.
Solution
Having a separate Instagram image section separate from the app’s carousel or video section and a button to let the user visit the other user’s Instagram profile.

Step Six

Wireframes on Paper.
Quickly sketched the layout with paper and pen. This helped me understand the functionality of the mobil application. It also helped me understand the connection between the information architecture or the site map and the visual design.
The instagram image section has a grid of 3 - 4 or 5 rounded images on one screen and can be rotated till 10 image slides complete. The 10 images can be selected while linking the instagram to the profile. After clicking on the image a pop up (top right) screen opens to display the image with vital information such as name, date posted. The mutual friends section has a grid of 3-4 or 5 circular images on one screen or depending upon the mutual friends (bottom left). If more more mutual friends are there then a slider will help slide the frame to next 3-4 or 5 images or so on. The section will be directly below the 30 seconds introduction video. The excess information on the page will be replaced with an one time 30 sec introductory video talking about her/him (top left). A pop will appear once you click the video and the video will be played, on closing the pop up, the pop up will dissolve as the previous screen will resurface. On request, life events can be a game changer, where you can link facebook to the events to show important events in your life and can be edited as well (top right). The circular events will be displayed below the mutual friends and above the Instagram image section. It will have an image in a circular shape with a tag below it. A pop will appear to if the life event is clicked and image will be enlarged wit the vital information such as place the photo is taken, tag and date at which it has been posted. Step Seven

High-fi wireframes.

Step Eight

Sitemap of the app to know what page comes after the previous page.
Before working on the UI design, I made the Information Architecture or sitemap chart to help understand the structure of the application.

Step Nine

Take a few Guerrilla tests to know the flaws in the design. Its a 15 minutes test where you ask the required questions to know the flaws in the design. The scenario was: To see the life events navigate through it. The user test was done with the two participants (male).
Findings - Moderate
“The life events needs to have large circular image with dotes in between to show connectivity”
“Need to lose the section blocks and replace it with small line to differentiate the section”
Findings - Moderate
“I liked the introductory video, makes it easy to grasp information”
“Almost everything I need is there”
“simpler UI”
“I like the colors, gives a good feel to the UI”
“Like the life events section”

Step Ten

Making the High-fi User Interface using the suggestions after the Guerrilla test.
I used the Sketch, Affinity Designer application for design. While creating the style guide and hi-fi mockups, I kept in mind visual design principles like:
Visibility
Proximity
Contrast
Alignment
Grid and Alignment
Scale
Space
Typography
Color Palette
Updated designs
Based on the feedback gathered from the tests, I redesigned certain aspects of the application to improve the user experience. Added depth to the icons on the screen. Added drop shadow to the images to give a depth feeling.

Shareholders of the App

Of course, everybody can use this app in their own way but our main audience would be mostly Indian people in the community, who have access to cell phones, social media or computers. We feel they are the most susceptible that there is no sure way to distinguish normal carousel to instagram images, cannot see mutual friends for now even if they see it, the window or the design is too small, there is too much written information on the screen. Although we are targeting younger generation who are looking for life partners or dating, this app can also be useful to older people who want to retry their luck in thi department. After gathering information to develop a working prototype, a task walk-through will be conducted at several stages of the design, firstly to measure the intuitive look and feel of the user interface and then the actual functionality. We believe that by observing users at various design stages, we can get firsthand insight about how well they respond to the design.
